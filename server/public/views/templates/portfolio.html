in another life...

My pre-coding career had some decent highlights. I am so thankful to everyone who gave me the opportunities to walk the road that has led me here. Some of my favorite moments, immortalized online:

on stage at e3


This was fun. I got to present the game I was working on at the time - Ashes of the Singularity - to a live theater audience and of course a gajillion livestream viewers.

game dev


I built the vast majority of the single-player content for Stardock's Ashes of the Singularity, a sci-fi real-time strategy game for Windows. Learning (and later extending the functionality of) the custom XML parser that the engine uses to create game scripts was a, well, a unique challenge. I'm extremely proud of what I was able to design and implement.

Ashes was always intended to be a 'skirmish' game (where two or more evenly matched players battle across symmetrical maps), but I made some pretty darn cool asymmetric single-player scenarios nonetheless.
